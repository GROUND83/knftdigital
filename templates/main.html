The Korean Alphabet

Hangul

Hangul is the alphabet of the written Korean language. It is comprised of 24 letters (originally 28), including 14 consonants and 10 vowels. Consonants are represented by curved or angled lines and vowels consist of a single vertical or horizontal line with short perpendicular lines on one side.

Korean calligraphy

Korean calligraphy is the art of freehand Hangul script. This style of art breathes life into Hangul’s geometric alphabet by adding soft curves and natural movement. Calligraphy turns words into stories and evokes style and emotion, and K-NFT exhibits the work of talented Korean artists who find and create beauty with Hangul calligraphy.
